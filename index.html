The Problem

The new licensing regime is crushing small crypto firms

💸

Expensive external help

Small teams rely on lawyers and consultants who charge by the hour — most of whom have never done a CASP application before. Costs add up fast.

📂

Scattered across 5+ tools

Policies in Google Drive. Reviews in email. Trackers in spreadsheets. Responsibilities in someone's head. Nothing connects.

⚠️

One missing file = months of delay

A single outdated policy, an unsigned declaration, or a missing procedure can send the entire application back to the queue.

🏴

Some startups just give up

The compliance burden is so high that teams of 3–15 people cannot sustain it. They leave the EU market or shut down.

Why Now

The clock is already running. Thousands of firms are not ready.

30 December 2024
MiCA became applicable for CASPs across the EU. The old VASP registration era is officially over.
Source: ESMA MiCA page
Now — transition window
In some jurisdictions, legacy providers have until 1 July 2026 to get authorized or lose market access. That is months away.
Source: CSSF MiCA page
After July 2026
No authorization = no operations. Only fully licensed CASPs can serve EU customers. The door closes.
⏱ Firms that are not actively preparing right now may not make it.
The reality for small teams right now
